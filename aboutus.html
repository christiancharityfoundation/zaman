Bruner W. Robert

Robert is the C.E.O. He is an American-Canadian international philanthropist and major player in the junior mining industry. Since the early 1980s, he has specialized in securing funding for the exploration and development of mineral and energy resources and advanced technology ventures. He is the founder and Chairman of his private, family-owned firm, Bruner Capital Corporation, which is active in global capital markets and has an established focus on emerging markets. Bruner is the founder of Bruner Mines.

Robert Bruner was born in Rhode Island, Newport, the eldest of three children born to immigrant parents. Bruner's father survived three years in Auschwitz while his mother worked as a forced laborer during the Holocaust. He graduated in 1974 from Reed College, Oregon, USA, with an Oil & Gas bachelor's degree. His senior year at Reed, Robert was student body president.

Robert has been with Christian Charity Foundation since 2008. He spearhead one of the development of CCF centre in Africa and also part of the CCF's Donation Spree Program, which requires of it members to donate to 10 charity homes in Africa.
